The Land That Time Forgot

Tucked between the Mediterranean and the Sahara, Daria (Dahar) is southeastern Tunisia's best-kept secret.

Daria ⵣ — also known as the Dahar mountains — is a rugged, breathtaking region in southeastern Tunisia. For thousands of years, Berber (Amazigh) communities have carved their lives into these mountains, building troglodyte homes, fortified ksour, and terraced farms called jessour that capture every drop of rain.

In 2026, Daria became Tunisia's first UNESCO Global Geopark — a living museum of 250 million years of geological history, dinosaur footprints, and ancient cave dwellings. It's a place where Star Wars was filmed, where olive trees outlive empires, and where hospitality is a religion.

Daria Decoded

Mind-blowing facts about this corner of the world that punches way above its weight.

🦕

Dinosaur Central

Daria is home to Tataouinea hannibalis — a dinosaur species named after Tataouine. Yes, that Tataouine that inspired Star Wars.

🏠

Living in Caves

Locals have lived in troglodyte homes for centuries. Naturally cool in summer, warm in winter, like something from a fantasy novel.

🪐

Star Wars Land

George Lucas filmed Luke Skywalker's home planet Tatooine right here. The ksour and desertscapes became a galaxy far, far away.

🌿

Jessour Magic

Ancient Berber farmers built jessour — terraced dams that trap rainwater in arid mountains. Genius engineering that's fed families for millennia.

🧵

Rainbow Weaving

Daria's women weave the most colorful carpets in North Africa. Each pattern tells a story — marriage, harvest, or just a really good joke.

🫒

Olive Gold

Some olive trees here are over 2,000 years old. They were planted when Hannibal was still marching elephants through the Alps.
